# When Convenience Creates Security Risk

## Scenario

A team member is assigned to a project that requires temporary access to multiple applications and systems.

To avoid delays, the necessary permissions are granted quickly so the employee can begin working immediately.

The project is completed successfully, but the permissions remain active long after they are needed.

Months later, neither the employee nor the organization has a clear understanding of why those permissions still exist.

---

## The Problem

Granting access is often treated as a one-time task.

However, access requirements change as projects end, responsibilities shift, and employees move between teams.

Without periodic reviews, users can accumulate permissions that no longer align with their current role.

Over time, this creates a gap between what access is required and what access is available.

---

## Security Impact

Excessive permissions can introduce several risks:

- Increased exposure to sensitive information
- Greater impact if an account is compromised
- Difficulty identifying appropriate access levels
- Reduced visibility during audits and reviews
- Higher likelihood of accidental or unauthorized actions

While the original access may have been justified, the lack of review creates unnecessary risk.

---

## Root Cause Analysis

The issue is rarely caused by technology.

More often, it originates from process gaps such as:

- No defined access review schedule
- Lack of ownership for access validation
- Incomplete documentation
- Temporary access becoming permanent
- Business priorities favoring convenience over governance

---

## Recommended Controls

Organizations can reduce this risk through:

### Access Reviews

Periodically validate user permissions against current responsibilities.

### Least Privilege

Grant only the minimum level of access required to perform assigned tasks.

### Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

Align permissions with defined roles rather than individual exceptions.

### Access Expiration

Automatically remove temporary permissions after a predetermined period.

### Ownership and Accountability

Assign responsibility for reviewing and approving access rights.

---

## Functional Analyst Perspective

This type of risk demonstrates how security issues often begin long before a security incident occurs.

The challenge is not simply granting access—it is ensuring access remains aligned with business needs over time.

As a Functional Analyst, I see access governance as a continuous process that requires collaboration between business stakeholders, system owners, and technical teams.

Strong security is not only about preventing unauthorized access.

It is also about ensuring authorized access remains appropriate.

---

## Key Takeaway

Security risks are not always introduced through malicious activity.
