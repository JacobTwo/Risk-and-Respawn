# The Security Risks of Uncontrolled Change

## Scenario

A new feature, process, or system update is scheduled for deployment.

The organization is under pressure to deliver quickly, and the change moves forward without completing all recommended validation activities.

Documentation is incomplete, stakeholder communication is limited, and security considerations are assumed rather than verified.

The deployment is successful initially, but unexpected issues begin to appear shortly afterward.

---

## The Problem

Changes are often viewed as operational activities rather than security events.

However, every modification to a system, process, application, or access model introduces the possibility of unintended consequences.

When changes occur without proper review and validation, organizations increase the likelihood of introducing new risks into their environment.

---

## Security Impact

Uncontrolled or poorly managed changes can lead to:

- Unauthorized access
- Misconfigured systems
- Data exposure
- Service disruptions
- Audit findings
- Compliance violations
- Reduced visibility into system behavior

In many cases, the issue is not the change itself but the absence of a structured process to evaluate its impact.

---

## Root Cause Analysis

Several factors commonly contribute to change-related security issues:

- Incomplete requirements
- Insufficient testing
- Lack of stakeholder involvement
- Missing documentation
- Inadequate communication
- Time pressure and accelerated delivery schedules
- Security reviews being skipped or treated as optional

These gaps often originate during planning rather than implementation.

---

## Recommended Controls

### Change Approval Process

Establish formal review and approval procedures before implementation.

### Impact Assessment

Evaluate operational, technical, and security implications prior to deployment.

### Stakeholder Validation

Ensure affected teams participate in the review process.

### Documentation Standards

Maintain clear records of requirements, decisions, and implementation details.

### Security Review Checkpoints

Include security considerations as part of the standard change lifecycle.

### Post-Implementation Review

Assess whether the change achieved its objective without introducing unintended risks.

---

## Functional Analyst Perspective

One of the most important lessons I have learned is that security should not be treated as a separate activity performed after implementation.

Security considerations should be integrated throughout the change lifecycle.

As a Functional Analyst, I often work at the point where business requirements become technical solutions. This position provides an opportunity to identify potential risks early, clarify expectations, and ensure that stakeholders understand the broader impact of proposed changes.

Many security incidents are not caused by sophisticated attacks.

They are caused by assumptions that were never challenged and changes that were never fully evaluated.

---

## Key Takeaway

Every change introduces risk.

The goal of change management is not to eliminate change but to ensure changes are implemented responsibly, understood by stakeholders, and evaluated from both operational and security perspectives.

Organizations that manage change effectively are often better positioned to manage security effectively as well.
